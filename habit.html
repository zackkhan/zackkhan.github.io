Habits

I believe the most important skill one can develop is habit building, because its the gift that keeps on giving.

Habits are like superpowers. When I first heard that my friend reads over one hundred books a year, I was surprised how anyone could do that. But its just about consistency and priorities. Habits turn the things that are on hard mode at first into easy mode over time, conserving our willpower to focus on new things that come up in life. It puts the necessities, what needs to get done everyday, in the background and ensures they are handled.

The problem is that as a young person, your life is bound by school deadlines and random texts from your friends making last-minute plans. With habits, you often have to have full control of your schedule, and that is a privilege. On top of that, you're still trying to figure out yourself, and thus, your priorities often change as well.

I was only able to take habit building seriously after I finished my degree at UMD. One thing I am actively trying to improve on is thinking more long-term, and habits are long-term by nature. Staying up all night to finish a feature isn't productive long term, but tackling it for an hour everyday is (still trying to grow out of the hackathon mindset)

Its only been a few weeks but here are some tips that have helped me along the way:

Don't quit before you see the benefits

We often quit habits long before we see the benefits. This defeats the purpose and throws away all the progress you've made. You'll be able to more easily stick with a habit once you start seeing the positive effect it has had on your life.

Log your habits

Keep a spreadsheet of your habits (whether you did them or not) for each day. Set a reminder (I use google calendar) for updating your spreadsheet for the next day.

Use Triggers

If you have a bad habit you want to get rid of, replace it with a good habit that responds to the same trigger as the bad one (ex: If I grind my teeth when I'm stressed, recognize when you are stressed (the trigger) and try meditation instead) Triggers can be used to remind you to do good habits such as drinking green tea as soon as you wake up.

Don't Skip Two Days

Life happens, and we end up skipping a day of a habit. That's fine, but skipping two days is death. If you ever skip, put all your willpower into picking it up again the next day.

I highly recommend reading Superhero by Habit for more tips on habit-building + some ideas for habits to build.

Habits I'm building for 2019:

  • Daily Prayer + Meditation
  • Sleep at 11, Wake up at 6 every day + Green Tea in the morning
  • Exercise every other day
  • Daily Journaling + Writing (like this blog!)
  • Reading everyday (at least 50 books this year)
Would love to hear what habits you are working on!
